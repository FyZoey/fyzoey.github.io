The Cursed Eva

According to the Old Testament, God created Adam in his image, and then created the woman Eve out of Adam's rib. Later, they were condemned and exiled by God for their original sin of stealing the forbidden fruit. God has put a curse on women: "You will love your husband, and he will rule over you." And many women from ancient times have indeed been cursed to fall behind their husbands, indulging in domestic work all day long, and giving up the opportunity to pursue life's value and more possibilities. The reflection on female identity and social role based on this phenomenon led me to complete the work. Rubber

The use of curses touched many cultures, and in ancient India, Persia, Egypt, Africa, and Europe, casting wax figures was a common method, which is still used today. Fungi are a kind of organisms that live by the way of saprophytic parasitism. They like dark humid corners, with rich bright colors and most of them are poisonous.

I chose three items that housewives often use in their daily housework to create, namely rubber gloves, disposable masks and cleaning sponges. I treated these three items in the same way as used ancient witchcraft to make curse dolls: first I filled them with cotton to help shape the later period, then I wound and tied them with cotton thread to make them in a twisted state, then I painted them with white paint, then wrapped them in layers of white paraffin, and finally nailed them on the main body. I used metal color-in process and bend the nails to make them look like mushrooms.

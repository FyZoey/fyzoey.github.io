MAResonance

E.O.Wilson, the founder of sociobiology, said that "genes control culture" , and memes, as the unit of cultural information transmission, develop in an organic way similar to the way genes are passed on. Explicit memes determine the externalization and prosperity of culture in different ages. As a genetic factor, recessive memes exist but do not necessarily appear. They do not gain the upper hand again until the right time comes. The culture they carry has been continuously reproduced, and each time after being reproduced appears similar but different from the last time. They show different styles in different times and environments. What is the same is that they all come from the same source and affect people's spiritual and material world at the same time. Starting from hippie culture and the counterculture behind it, this project studies the meme inheritance process under the appearance of retro culture becoming the fashion restoring and its influence on social development.

Flowers are one of the most common elements of the counterculture.

During the counterculture movement around the 1960s, hippies referred to themselves as "flower children," wearing garlands, holding bouquets, shouting slogans about love and peace, and sticking flowers into guns became iconic images in the course of history.

Buddhist culture first emerged to reflect on the imperfections of the prevailing Hindu culture. The Buddha's life was also inextricably linked to the flower, which symbolizes beauty and holiness. Buddha was born, flowers bloom, walk seven steps, step by step birth lotus, into the taoist doctrine, heaven flowers praise. People also used precious flowers to feed the Buddha.

In the counter-cultural movement at the present times, flowers were still immortal symbols, and people endowed flowers with beautiful images such as hope and eternity. No matter in variety or color, flowers or totems, they frequently appeared, indicating people's expectation for the present life and future development.

Nature

Resin | Paper | Gold Foil | Copper | Pearl | Shell | Natural Stone

175*120*30mm

2019

Nature is the foundation and eternal theme of the counterculture movement.

They seem to be disgusted with the society, abandon the society and go into the nature of the wilderness, but in fact they yearn for a free, peaceful and vibrant life.

With the progress of science and technology comes environmental pollution, reduction of biodiversity and frequent natural disasters. Natural handicrafts and rural life have become favored objects of many people. Abandoning the extravagant material enjoyment and the value concept of eager for quick success and instant profit, the pursuit of spiritual enrichment and more pure and natural beauty has become people's appeal.

Young people who support the counterculture movement express their rebellion and dissatisfaction with society in a seemingly casual way.

I looked at Nepalese Buddhist culture, hippie culture and modern counter-traditional culture as the results of three stages of genetic inheritance, compared and studied, and finally summarized five most significant key points to present my understanding of this phenomenon.

The finished works can be worn as brooches, and due to the adoption of single point fixation, two-point fixation and three-point fixation respectively, some pieces can be moved.

The hippie movement, which originated in the West in the 1960s, was a movement to express dissatisfaction with the real world by moving away from mainstream society. The hippies' strange clothes, rock music, psychedelics, communal life and artistic activities all highlighted their personality and rebellion, and these spiritual thoughts and behavior habits all had a profound influence on later generations. The hippies' boldly mixed style of dress was usually casual and colorful, with flowers, tassels, beads and tie-dye being the most common elements.

The Buddhist culture of Nepal is the main source of hippie culture, whose essence in the initial rise is to rebel against the mainstream traditional culture. It is reflected in philosophical concepts, life attitudes, especially in clothing and accessories. The gemstone red coral, turquoise, lapis lazuli, agate, Tridacna, amber, crystal, pearl, colored glaze and other natural stones in Buddhist culture are also often worn by hippies.

Until today, we can still find traces of hippie culture in our life. In addition to the hippie-style clothes worn by people who love the retro culture, some people joined in the array of counterculture, just like the hippies at the beginning, to fight against all the bad phenomena that need to be abandoned. They long for spiritual freedom and peace of life, and pursue a more perfect themselves and a more ideal world.

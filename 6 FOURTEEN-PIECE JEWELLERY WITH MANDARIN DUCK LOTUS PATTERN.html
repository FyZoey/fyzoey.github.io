FOURTEEN-PIECE JEWELLERY WITH MANDARIN DUCK LOTUS PATTERN

"Zhi Bei" is an ornament used by ancient Chinese women to stick on the head comb. It is usually gold, curved and decorated with traditional patterns. The gold Zhi Bei is decorated by wrapping the gold pieces with decorative patterns on the wood comb back, making the wood comb embedded in the gold cover, and then wearing it on the head bun to achieve the decorative effect. This project starts from the study of a golden Zhi Bei with the pattern of mandarin duck lotus in Ming Dynasty, and finally creates a set of modern jewelry with the pattern of mandarin duck lotus.

The golden comb Zhi Bei, a traditional Chinese headpiece, has been popular since the Tang Dynasty, mainly in the shape of crescent moon, but also in the shape of horizontal flag, trapezoid, etc. The ornamentation is mainly plant grain, supplemented by animal grain, geometric grain and so on. Among them, lotus flower is endowed with the noble character of "rising from the mud without staining, ripening without being evil" . It is a symbol of holiness in Buddhist culture and a model of cultural exchange between China and foreign countries. Together with lotus seed, lotus flower has the meaning of "giving birth to a noble son" , which is widely loved in ancient China. Mandarin ducks often appear together with them, used to describe the conjugal love, as the saying goes, "Only the envy of mandarin ducks do not envy fairy". The piece of jewellery I chose was a golden comb back with mandarin duck and lotus pattern.

The golden Zhi Bei is usually cut and shaped with gold pieces, and patterns are added by welding gold beads or chisels. The hollow body of the comb is used to insert comb teeth. Its essence is to use the "cover" way to make the flat pattern become a kind of decoration, I also use this concept in my own design.

I drew the pattern of Mandarin duck lotus according to the method of Yin and Yang engraving in traditional Chinese paper cutting. I printed the gold pigment on the transparent PVC material with the tool of silkscreen printing, and cut, punched and coated with oil by hand with leather. The final product has a total of 14 pieces, which are used to cover the neck, ears, fingers and hair bun respectively. All of them use simple linear cutting of the flat PVC, so that the planar pattern can act on the three-dimensional body for decoration.
